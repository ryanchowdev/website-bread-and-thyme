Bread & Thyme

Meet Our Founders

Emily Foster

Emily Foster's passion for baking was sparked in her grandmother's kitchen, where the aroma of freshly baked bread mingled with stories of old-world baking traditions. Growing up, Emily spent countless hours by her grandmother's side, learning the art of kneading dough and crafting artisanal bread with love and care. Her grandmother's tales of family recipes and baking techniques from a bygone era inspired Emily to dream of opening her own bakery one day. After years of working in the corporate world, Emily realized that her heart belonged in the kitchen, and she was determined to turn her passion into a reality.

Jacob Meyers

Jacob Meyers' culinary journey began with his extensive travels around the world, where he immersed himself in diverse cultures and their unique culinary practices. Fascinated by the myriad of flavors and techniques he encountered, Jacob began collecting recipes and honing his skills as a baker and chef. His travels introduced him to the rich world of herbs and spices, with thyme quickly becoming his favorite for its ability to add depth and character to any dish. Jacob's adventurous spirit and passion for innovative cooking brought a unique flair to Bread & Thyme.

The Vision

Bread & Thyme was more than just a business venture; it was a place where tradition met innovation. The bakery's philosophy was simple: use the finest ingredients, bake with love, and create a warm, welcoming atmosphere for everyone who walked through the door. The centerpiece of their offerings was the sourdough bread, baked fresh every morning. Emily's grandmother's recipe, with a touch of Jacob's secret blend of herbs, quickly became a local favorite. Alongside the bread, they offered a variety of pastries, each with a unique twist reflecting Jacob's culinary adventures. As Bread & Thyme flourished, Emily and Jacob expanded their menu with artisanal sandwiches and seasonal specials, delighting customers and establishing the bakery as a go-to destination for food enthusiasts.

Community and Sustainability

From the outset, Bread & Thyme committed to sustainability and supporting local farmers. They sourced their flour from a nearby organic farm and used seasonal produce for their fillings and toppings, ensuring freshness and strengthening community bonds. Emily and Jacob's dedication to sustainable practices extended to eco-friendly packaging and encouraging customers to bring their own containers. Additionally, they hosted popular baking classes and workshops, fostering a sense of community and inspiring a new generation of bakers. This commitment to sustainability and community-centered activities further solidified Bread & Thyme's reputation as a beloved local establishment.
