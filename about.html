Bread & Thyme

Meet Our Founders

Emily Foster

Emily had always been enchanted by her grandmother's stories of old-world baking. As a child, she would spend hours in the kitchen, kneading dough and listening to tales of artisanal bread, crafted with love and care.

Jacob Meyers

Jacob, on the other hand, had traveled extensively, collecting recipes and techniques from different cultures. He was fascinated by the use of herbs and spices, particularly thyme, which he believed added a unique depth to any dish.

The Vision

Bread & Thyme was more than just a business venture; it was a place where tradition met innovation. The bakery's philosophy was simple: use the finest ingredients, bake with love, and create a warm, welcoming atmosphere for everyone who walked through the door. The centerpiece of their offerings was the sourdough bread, baked fresh every morning. Emily's grandmother's recipe, with a touch of Jacob's secret blend of herbs, quickly became a local favorite. Alongside the bread, they offered a variety of pastries, each with a unique twist, reflecting Jacob's culinary adventures.

Community and Sustainability

From the outset, Bread & Thyme committed to sustainability and supporting local farmers. They sourced their flour from a nearby organic farm and used seasonal produce for their fillings and toppings. This not only ensured the freshness of their products but also strengthened their bond with the community.
